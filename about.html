ABOUT US

Founded in 1897, the Animal Lifeline-Durban & Coast along with the SPCA is one of South Africa's oldest and most respected animal welfare organisations. Animal Lifeline is a separate entity but works closely with the SPCA to ensure that all animals in need receive the best possible care and attention. As a registered non-profit (NPO 002-312) and a Public Benefit Organisation (PBO 930001302), we have dedicated over a century to rescuing, rehabilitating, and rehoming animals in KwaZulu-Natal. Our mission is to prevent cruelty to animals, rescue those in need, and provide them with shelter, medical care, and love. We aim to educate the public about responsible pet ownership, promote animal welfare, and ensure that every animal has the chance to live in a safe and loving environment.

Our History:

The Animal Lifeline movement began in England in 1824, and in South Africa, the Cape of Good Hope SPCA was founded in 1872 then Animal Lifeline. The Animal Lifeline-Durban & Coast and SPCA has been operational since 1897, growing from a small group of passionate animal lovers into a large organisation that handles thousands of cases of animal cruelty, neglect, and abandonment every year. We have remained a trusted community resource for over 125 years.Far more than just an animal shelter and adoption facility, the Animal Lifeline plays a humanising role in society, educating people, especially children about the rights of all living creatures, and working for better legal protection for animals (particularly farm animals) and more severe penalties for perpetrators of cruelty.

What we Do?

  • Rescue and rehabilitate abused, neglected, or abandoned animals
  • Provide safe shelter, food, and veterinary care
  • Offer adoption programs to match animals with loving families
  • Investigate and prosecute cases of animal cruelty
  • Educate the public about responsible pet ownership and animal welfare
  • Conduct community outreach and awareness programs

Our Facilities

Our modern facilities in Springfield Park include animal shelters, a veterinary clinic, adoption centers, and educational outreach spaces. These facilities allow us to provide high-quality care for every animal we rescue and prepare them for a successful transition into loving homes.
