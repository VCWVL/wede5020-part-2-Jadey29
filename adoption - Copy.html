Why Adopt?

Thousands of animals are abandoned every year. By adopting, you help reduce overpopulation and give a deserving animal a second chance at life. Our adoption fees cover sterilization, vaccinations, and a vet check-up, giving you peace of mind and saving you costs in the long run.

Choosing to adopt a pet is one of the most rewarding decisions you can make. By adopting, you are giving an abandoned or abused animal a chance at a new, loving home. Every adoption helps us continue our mission of preventing cruelty and promoting kindness to animals.


Adoption Process

Here is how our adoption process works:

Once the below form is filled, these are the remaining steps for the adoption process:

Firstly, fill this form with all required details.


  • Next, arrange a meeting with an adoption counselor to discuss your lifestyle, preferences, and the specific needs of the pet you are considering.
  • The adoption counselor is Caroline Smith. You can get her details and book a meeting by filling out the enquiry webpage. Please be specific about what you request from us.
  • After that process, you will be called in to meet the pets. Visit the shelter to meet the animals available for adoption. Spend time interacting with them to find the best match for you. In this meeting you will be given forms with the adoption pricing.
  • Then a Home Visit (if necessary): In some cases, SPCA locations may require a home visit to ensure that your living environment is safe and suitable for the pet.
  • Once every thing is up to standard, there is an adoption Fee: Pay the adoption fee, which can vary by location. This fee usually covers vaccinations, spaying/neutering, and microchipping. This will all take place at our facility in Springfield.

  • Lastly,Finalizing Adoption: Complete the adoption process by signing the adoption contract and providing identification.This document will be handed to you in person. You can then take your new pet home, along with any essential supplies and information.
  • Post-Adoption Support: After adoption, you can access resources and support from the SPCA for any questions or concerns you may have. We also provide follow-up calls or visits to help ensure a smooth transition for you and your new pet.

Adoption Requirements

To adopt a pet from us, you must:

  • Be 18 years or older.
  • Provide proof of residence and identification.
  • Have consent from your landlord/body corporate (if renting).
  • Ensure existing pets are sterilized and vaccinated.
  • Show the ability to provide long-term care including food, shelter, and vet services.

By meeting these requirements, you help us ensure that our animals go to safe and loving homes.
